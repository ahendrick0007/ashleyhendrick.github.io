Prevention and Safety Measures

Staying safe during a tornado requires preparedness and awareness. The following are different ways to stay safe based on your location:

At Home

  • Have a Plan: Create a family tornado plan, including identifying a safe room or shelter in your home, such as a basement or an interior room on the lowest floor without windows.
  • Prepare an Emergency Kit: Keep an emergency kit with essentials such as water, non-perishable food, flashlight, batteries, and a first aid kit.
  • Stay Informed: Monitor weather forecasts and warnings using a NOAA Weather Radio or a reliable weather app on your smartphone.
  • Practice Drills: Conduct tornado drills with your family to ensure everyone knows what to do during a tornado warning.

At Work or School

  • Know the Plan: Familiarize yourself with the tornado safety plan at your workplace or school, including evacuation routes and designated safe areas.
  • Stay Alert: Pay attention to weather updates and heed tornado warnings issued by authorities.
  • Follow Instructions: Follow instructions from emergency personnel and evacuate to designated safe areas immediately.
  • Account for Others: If responsible for others, such as students or coworkers, ensure everyone is accounted for and safely evacuated.

Outdoors or in a Vehicle

  • Seek Shelter Immediately: If outdoors, seek shelter in a sturdy building or lie flat in a low-lying area and cover your head with your hands.
  • Avoid Vehicles: Do not try to outrun a tornado in a vehicle. Instead, abandon the vehicle and seek shelter in a nearby building or lie flat in a ditch.
  • Stay Informed: Pay attention to weather updates and take shelter if a tornado warning is issued for your location.
  • Be Prepared: Keep a portable emergency kit in your vehicle with essentials such as water, snacks, a flashlight, and a blanket.
