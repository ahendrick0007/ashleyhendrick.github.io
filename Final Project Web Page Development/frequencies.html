Frequencies of Tornadoes

Tornadoes occur more frequently in certain regions and during specific times of the year.

Factors such as geography, climate, and atmospheric conditions influence the frequency of tornado occurrences.

In regions known as "Tornado Alley," tornadoes are more common due to the convergence of warm, moist air from the Gulf of Mexico and cool, dry air from Canada.

Statistically, Tornado Alley experiences a higher frequency of tornadoes compared to areas outside of Tornado Alley. However, tornadoes can still occur in other regions, albeit less frequently.

It's essential for residents and authorities in all areas to be prepared for tornadoes, regardless of their likelihood of occurrence.
